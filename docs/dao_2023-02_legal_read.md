# Legal Read: DAO 2023-02 (EPR IRR) Against the Thesis Model
**Source:** full text, DENR Administrative Order 2023-02, issued 24 Jan 2023, published 02 Feb 2023. Read against the manuscript's claims, assumption set A1-A4, and penalty model. Every point cites the section of the primary text.

## R1. Gas-energy-recovery credit: GROUNDED (was the manuscript's one conditional)
Sec. 16.3.3.4 names the creditable post-recovery dispositions: reuse, recycling, feedstock for coprocessing, **waste-to-fuel, waste-to-energy**, other applications allowed by law, or SLF disposal. Sec. 12.2.2.2 places both chemical recycling to monomers (12.2.2.2.4) and waste-to-energy (12.2.2.2.7, "pursuant to standards and guidelines set forth by the Department") in the diversion hierarchy. Pyrolysis-to-liquid = waste-to-fuel by name; the fuel-gas module = waste-to-energy by name. Residual caveat: the Department's WtE standards are directed but pending, which the manuscript now states as the most consequential pending rule. Nuance handled: Sec. 6.59 excludes incineration from resource recovery and Sec. 5.4 from ESWM best practice; combusting a pyrolysis-derived fuel gas is use of a derived fuel, not incineration of solid waste, a distinction the rules themselves draw.

## R2. Rigid/Flexible segregated crediting: NEW constraint structure (Sec. 16.3.3.3)
"Flexibles cannot be used to offset Rigids and vice versa," regardless of brand. Category mapping for our DKR set: PE_film, PP_film, MULTILAYER, CLOGGED -> Flexibles (Sec. 13(1)); PE_rigid, PP_rigid, PET -> Rigids (13(2)); polystyrene is its own packaging class (13(4)); ECAR audit groups Flexibles = 13(1),(3),(4) and Rigids = 13(2) with PS treatment ambiguous in the drafting (19.1.2.1). Model consequence: our single aggregate constraint approximates the binding flexible-side obligation for a flexible-dominated stream; the two-class constraint is a named next-generation refinement (now in the manuscript's Methods and Limitations).

## R3. Penalty structure: verified, and F2 SHARPENED (Sec. 21.1)
Tiers confirmed: first offense 5-10 MPHP, second 10-15, third 15-20; and for missed 44-F targets, the same fines OR twice the cost of recovery and diversion of the shortfall, whichever is higher (methodology for that cost to be issued by the Bureau/NEC, Sec. 9.2.4). The teeth: **third offense carries automatic suspension of the business permit until compliance.** For the paper: monetary fines remain two orders below network profit (F2 stands), but the existential permit-suspension instrument is exactly what an expected-cost objective cannot price, which completes the argument that chance-constrained design is what an obliged enterprise rationally buys. Collectives/PROs that miss targets must identify non-performing members or all members become liable (21.2), pushing the incidence onto enterprises.

## R4. Other model-relevant provisions
Registration-year floor (16.3.3.2): registering in 2024 starts at 40%, 2025 at 50%, not 20%: strengthens the manuscript's ramp-dominance argument. Footprint = verifiable kilograms brought to market, disclosed by type Rigid/Flexible, not by brand (16.3.2). Diversion accomplishments require notarized Sworn Plastic Waste Diversion Certificates aggregated in the ECAR (16.3.3.5-16.3.3.6): the network operator is a certificate-issuing waste diverter (6.73). Geographic roll-out plan expected in EPR programs, Metro Manila first (16.3.6.2): matches the study instance. WACS guidelines exist under NSWMC Resolution 1380 s.2020 for clean-up quantification (12.2.4.2): the hook for the resin-resolved characterization the thesis recommends. Fiscal side (Sec. 20.2.1): EPR expenses are tax-deductible and SIPP incentives may apply: an upside not in the TEA, noted for P6 backlog.
