Dear Editor,

Please consider our manuscript, "Chance-constrained design of decentralized plastic-pyrolysis networks under feedstock-composition uncertainty: an EPR-compliance study for the Philippines," for publication as a full-length article in Computers & Chemical Engineering.

The paper sits squarely in the journal's core territory of process systems engineering for waste valorization; CACE recently published deterministic siting of mixed-plastic pyrolysis networks (Crîstiu et al., 2024, CACE 180:108503), and our study is the natural next step in that line. We couple a validated pyrolysis and fractionation process model, a calibrated Gaussian-process surrogate embedded exactly in the network program, and a two-stage stochastic spatial superstructure in which the recovery obligation of the Philippine Extended Producer Responsibility Act appears in its statute-faithful, footprint-based form.

Three results will interest your readers beyond the application. First, compliance risk turns out to be chemistry-bound: a network processing its entire contracted footprint meets the 80% obligation in only 24 to 30% of composition scenarios, no siting decision changes this, and one gas-energy-recovery module raises compliance to 80%. Second, the value of the stochastic solution is zero to within solver tolerance, for economic reasons we identify and argue are generic to profit-dominant valorization networks under compliance obligations; the decision-relevant quantity is instead the cost of compliance assurance, which spans a factor of twenty between spatial-heterogeneity regimes and switches mechanism from hardware hedging to selective contracting. Third, feedstock composition carries 62% of the variance in net present value, more than all product prices and capital uncertainty combined, so the compliance analysis and the economics point independently at the same missing measurement: resin-resolved waste characterization.

The methodological chain is validated end to end: the yield model reproduces independent mixture experiments to 4.6 percentage points, the reduced flowsheet matches six rigorous simulations to within 2% under leave-one-out cross-validation, and the surrogate's predictive intervals achieve 0.99 to 1.00 empirical coverage. Every number in the paper, and all six figures, regenerate from a single archived repository (DOI to be inserted at acceptance).

The manuscript is original, has not been published previously, and is not under consideration elsewhere. All authors have approved the submission and declare no competing interests. Suggested reviewers are provided in the submission system.
